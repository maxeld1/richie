About Me

Not To Make It About Me...

My name is Richie. I am a composer, writer, and performer who’s been pretending to have it all figured out for years, with mixed results. I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Music Composition, which has given me a deep appreciation for harmony, counterpoint, and the financial instability of the arts. I’m a trained theatre performer and vocalist, with a background in both classical and contemporary performance. Over time, I’ve worked across music, theatre, and film — writing songs, scripts, and stories that explore humor, vulnerability, and the uncomfortable beauty of being human. My work often blends sincerity and satire, because I find that the line between “profound” and “ridiculous” is often thinner than we’d like to admit. As a writer and musician, I aim to create things that feel honest — equal parts heartfelt and self-aware — the kind of work that makes people laugh right before realizing they might cry. I’ve been called “versatile,” “dedicated,” and once, “a risk,” which feels about right.

Manifesto.

I create from the basement—literally and spiritually. My work lives in the cluttered, dim corners where people actually feel things, not where they pretend to. I write flawed humans, not heroic myths: people who joke too much, love imperfectly, spiral a little, and still try to do the right thing even when it hurts everyone involved. Comedy is my scalpel; I use laughter as the incision that makes truth survivable, revealing the mechanism of the trick while somehow preserving the magic. Music is my bloodstream, a way to navigate emotions that dialogue alone can’t carry. I work across mediums because stories don’t care about boundaries, and the form should always follow the spark. I believe inspiration is a kind of holy madness—uninvited, relentless, and necessary. My art lives in the fragile tension between what we feel and what we actually do, in the almosts and almost-said moments. I create for people who feel too much and hide it behind humor, for anyone who’s ever tried to joke through loneliness or navigate love without a map. Ultimately, I make things because creation is an act of revolt against silence, and because somewhere in the dark, someone is waiting for a story that lets them breathe again.
